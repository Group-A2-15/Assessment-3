Risks


These are some of the major risks we have identified:

  • Specific components of hardware or apps chosen do not perform as expected and need to be replaced – mitigated by early testing and market research for alternatives.

  • Technology is too complex for emergency workers (mostly volunteers) and citizens to learn and remember, particularly under stressful conditions. Mitigation – strong UX/UI work to ensure that end product will be more instinctive and contextual help built into system for reference in real time operations.

  • Enclosure for system and external components (microphone, camera, touchscreen, speaker) not being appropriately cooled (number and placement of heat sinks, fans). Mitigation – Heat stress testing in temperature-controlled environment to find maximum temperature unit can operate at. This will be coupled with experimentation to find the optimal temperature management. This heat testing would become part of the Quality Control processes in future commercial units.

  • Enclosure and external components not having enough insulation to operate in intense bushfire conditions. Mitigation - Heat stress testing in a temperature-controlled environment with varying types, placement and amounts of insulation placed in different units to find which is most effective. As mentioned above this heat stressing will eventually evolve into a crucial Quality Control process for each commercial unit produced.

  • Enclosure for system being too fragile (cracking/breaking when used whilst operating. Mitigation - Drop testing. Enclosures with dummy internals will be dropped from different heights on different surfaces. These test enclosures will be of varying types of plastic and percentage of glass fibre reinforcement. These will be coupled with a rubber outer layer for grip/shock absorption, these rubber outers will also be of varying thickness.

  • Enclosure not providing enough physical shock protection. Mitigation – Drop testing. Once optimal insulation, type of plastic, percentage, and preferred type of rubber outer is found. Internal shock-proofing may be integrated into live units with varying thicknesses of Rubber Outer to find which is most effective and ergonomic.
